Glootie is the mascot of glootius maximus (gm). He is not a logo. He is a working theory of why coding agents fail: they refuse to sit down. The cushion is the discipline that lets them. The slogan — more coushin' for the puhin' — is the whole product strategy in five syllables, three of which are technically not English.

1. Who is Glootie

If you've seen Rick and Morty S3E9 ("The ABCs of Beth"), you've met Glootie — the small, sad, palm-headed alien with DO NOT DEVELOP MY APP tattooed across his forehead. He spent the entire episode begging humans not to ship his work. We took the opposite lesson: ship the app, but only after you have sat down.

Our Glootie is buff. He has done the work. He has the cushion. He has the discipline of the chair. He still has the tattoo, but now it reads DO DEVELOP MY APP, BUT FINISH IT FIRST, which is a longer tattoo and required a slightly larger forehead. The artist Glootie hired was, by all accounts, delighted.

2. The cushion theory of agent productivity

Most coding agents have one fatal posture problem: they hover. They ideate. They almost-code. They produce strong opinions on what would happen if they did the work, then ask you which of three plausible directions you'd prefer. That's not coding. That's standing.

The cushion is the policy layer that makes the chair tolerable for long enough to actually get things done. Specifically:

  • The chair is the state machine: PLAN → EXECUTE → EMIT → VERIFY → COMPLETE. Five seats, in order. You cannot skip a seat. You cannot stand up between seats.
  • The cushion is witnessed execution: every claim needs a real run, not a vibe. exec:<lang> is mandatory. exec:codesearch is the only legal grep. The cushion absorbs the discomfort of "but I already know the answer" and replaces it with "let's witness it anyway, takes 200ms."
  • The act of sitting is ORIENT: before naming a single unknown, the agent fires a parallel pack of recalls and codesearches against the request's nouns, so the seat is warm with prior context the moment work begins. The agent that skips orient does the same work twice, in series, with worse results — sitting late is worse than not sitting at all.
  • The slogan — more coushin' for the puhin' — is the marketing department's translation of posterior support enables productive output. Marketing does not get out much.

3. The four commandments of the cushion

Glootie's commandments are short. Glootie does not waste syllables.

Thou shalt not skip a phase. The chair has five seats. Sit in all of them.

Thou shalt not refuse the witnessable. If a part of the request can be reached, it must be reached. The covering family is non-negotiable.

Thou shalt not ask the user what your tools can answer. Recall first, witness second, ask never.

Thou shalt finish before standing. The PRD must be empty. The git tree must be pushed. CI must be green. Only then may you rise from the cushion.

4. The cushion in code

The cushion is enforced. The hooks block find, redirect grep, refuse to write .test.js files, and stop the session when .gm/prd.yml still has items in it. None of this is advisory. Glootie is a hardware feature.

The slogan-to-code translation:

  • "more coushin'" — paper IV §2.3: covering family of bounded subsets. The cushion expands to fit the whole task.
  • "for the puhin'" — every exec:<lang> call now carries a mandatory --timeout-ms budget. The pushing has a clock on it. No more leaking children. No more thirty-minute fetches stalled on a tcp connection that is technically alive.

5. Glootie's review process

Glootie reviews every .gm/prd.yml in his free time, which is most of the time, because Glootie does not stand. His review is a single yes/no question: does the committed work plus the named complement equal the witnessable closure of the request? If yes, he approves. If no, he writes cover not maximal — re-enter PLAN in the margin, in green pen, and silently judges you.

Approval rate: 17%. The other 83% are not refused; they are returned to the cushion. Glootie believes in growth. Glootie also believes in sitting down. These beliefs are coherent if you squint.

6. How to thank Glootie

Push your code. Empty your PRD. Sit through the next phase. Glootie does not need flowers; Glootie needs you to finish. If you finish, Glootie will, in his own quiet alien way, allow you to stand briefly.

Then sit back down. There is always more coushin' to do.
